<objective>
Make a PSTN pickup feel intentional: on answer, play ONE short ring/ringback sample, then a short pre-rendered KPH "hey" clip that signals connection and prompts the caller to say the passphrase. Both cues are barge-in-able. Telephony answer/greeting flow ONLY.

Purpose: Today `on_stasis_start` answers and (in the production gated flow) keeps the caller silent/dark until the passphrase unlock — callers can't tell the line picked up and don't know they're expected to speak the phrase. This adds an intentional ring + a punchy KPH prompt using pre-rendered audio (reusing the render_greetings.py pattern), played through the existing telephony output transport so barge-in is free.

Output: A new pre-rendered `kph-hey.wav` asset + render script, a `telephony/pickup_cue.py` player (in-code ring synth + hey loader + barge-in-safe injection), and a two-line wiring into the controller answer path. The browser client is untouched.
</objective>
