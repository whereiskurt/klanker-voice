<objective>
Wire the ledger into the one seam every session flows through — `create_call_session()` — and guarantee the last turns survive teardown. This registers the two pipecat aggregator event handlers (user + assistant turns), constructs a `LedgerWriter` from the caller identity (email/code for browser, caller_id/did + mint-sub for PSTN), skips bypass/smoke sessions, final-flushes in `CallSession.run()`'s `finally`, and adds a FastAPI shutdown lifespan (+ telephony `__main__` finally) that drains all writers within a bounded timeout before ECS's 30s SIGKILL. One tap covers all three entry paths with zero changes at the three call sites.

Purpose: capture both sides of every conversation, on every channel, durably.
Output: `call_runtime.py` tap + writer field + final flush; `server.py` identity plumbing + shutdown drain; `controller.py` mint-sub return + PSTN identity + unlock-time writer enable; `telephony/__main__.py` drain.
</objective>
